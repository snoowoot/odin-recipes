Dad's Homemade Corn Dogs

I despise all the additives in store-bought corn dogs, so this recipe makes me happy!

Ingrediants

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 cup cold whole milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 16 large hot dogs
  • 2 cups vegetable oil for frying, or as needed
  • 16 skewers

Direction

  1. Combine cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and pepper in a bowl; stir in milk and egg until smooth. Cover and refrigerate until needed.
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Cook hot dogs in boiling water until heated through, about 7 minutes. Remove to a paper towel-lined plate and let rest for 10 minutes
  3. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Insert one skewer into each hot dog and blot dry if wet. Pour chilled batter into a tall drinking glass.
  4. Dip one hot dog into batter until well coated; transfer to hot oil. Repeat with as many hot dogs as can comfortably fit in the fryer without overcrowding (one or two more). Fry until lightly browned, about 3 minutes. Drain on paper towels. Repeat to coat and fry remaining hot dogs.

Tips

If you have more time, refrigerate the batter in Step 1 for up to an hour. A colder batter works better to coat the hot dogs.
